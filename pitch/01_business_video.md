# Business Video Script — 2 minutes

**Jury context:** Member of the Fintech Association of Hong Kong accelerator board. Has owned companies. Operator mindset, knows the HK ecosystem intimately. Not a technologist — speak to commercial pain and market fit.

**Format:** Hook on operator pain → Problem → Solution → HK fit → Business model → CTA
**Tone:** Peer-to-peer, grounded, no jargon. Speak to the person who's had to deal with accountants, audits, and cross-border complexity firsthand.

---

## [0:00–0:20] Hook — the operator pain

> "If you've run a company in Hong Kong with any international operations, you know the moment. Your accountant comes back and says: 'We might have a permanent establishment issue in Germany.' You don't know what that means. Your lawyers don't agree. Six weeks later you have a $50,000 bill and no clear answer.
>
> That's not an edge case. That's the standard experience for any HK-headquartered company with European or US operations."

---

## [0:20–0:50] The Problem

> "Cross-border tax exposure is invisible until it isn't. The rules exist — in tax treaties, in local statutes — but they live in different jurisdictions, in different documents, applied by advisors who each see only their piece.
>
> Nobody is looking at the whole picture at once. And the companies who get hurt aren't doing anything wrong — they just didn't know that 185 days of employee presence in France creates a taxable entity there, while Germany is simultaneously claiming the same profits.
>
> The tools that exist today were built for Big 4 firms. They assume you already know what you owe."

---

## [0:50–1:15] The Solution

> "Tributary gives any multinational the visibility that used to require a $50,000 engagement.
>
> You upload the financial exports your accountant already has. Our system maps your cross-border flows, applies the applicable treaties and local rules, and tells you where there's exposure — before audit, before it's a crisis.
>
> Every finding is cited to the specific statute. Every number is computed deterministically — not guessed by an AI. The AI writes the summary your advisor reads; the engine does the maths."

---

## [1:15–1:30] Why Hong Kong, why now

> "Hong Kong is the perfect starting point. It's the regional headquarters jurisdiction for most Asia-Pacific multinationals — sitting at the intersection of HK-EU and HK-US flows that generate exactly these conflicts.
>
> And the regulatory environment is shifting. OECD Pillar Two is real. The IRD is not asleep. The HK fintech sector — which the Fintech Association knows better than anyone — has an acute version of this: cross-border payment flows, digital asset treasury, multi-entity structures. Every one of those creates tax exposure that nobody is currently mapping in real time."

---

## [1:30–1:50] Business model

> "We sell to the CFO directly — SaaS subscription per entity per jurisdiction. No engagement letter, no hourly billing, no six-week wait. The advisor still reviews and signs off. We just make sure she arrives at that conversation with the right information already on the table.
>
> The rule library is the moat. Adding a new country is one configuration file — the engine never changes. We start HK-focused and expand jurisdiction coverage on customer demand."

---

## [1:50–2:00] CTA

> "Every company in this room with cross-border operations is either already exposed or doesn't know it yet. We built Tributary to close that gap — and we want to close it starting in Hong Kong."
