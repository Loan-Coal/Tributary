# Live Pitch — 2 min + 2 min Q&A (Round 1)

**Jury context:** Member of the Fintech Association of Hong Kong accelerator board. Has owned companies. Operator, not technologist. She knows what it costs to run a company, what accountants charge, what the IRD is like. Speak peer-to-peer.

**All team members must be present onsite.**
**Tip:** Have the web UI open and loaded before the pitch starts.

---

## Pitch Script (2 min)

### Hook (20s)
> "If you've run a company in Hong Kong with international operations, you've had the call. Your accountant rings and says there might be a permanent establishment issue in Germany. You don't know what that means. Your lawyers don't agree. Six weeks later you have a $50,000 bill and still no clear answer.
>
> That's not an edge case. That's the standard."

### Problem (25s)
> "Cross-border tax exposure is invisible until audit. The rules exist — in treaties, in local statutes — but they live across jurisdictions, applied by advisors who each see only their piece. Nobody looks at the whole picture at once.
>
> The companies that get hurt aren't doing anything wrong. They just didn't know that 185 days of employee presence in France creates a taxable entity there — while Germany is simultaneously claiming the same profits."

### Solution + demo (40s)
> "Tributary gives any multinational the visibility that used to require a Big 4 engagement."
>
> *(Show the web UI — click Run Analysis)*
>
> "You upload the financial data your accountant already has. We map your cross-border flows, apply the applicable treaties and local rules, and surface exposure before it becomes a crisis. Every finding is cited to the specific statute. Every number computed deterministically — not guessed by AI.
>
> Here — that's our engine detecting the PE Triangle in real time. Germany and France both claiming the same profits. Treaty resolution flagged. Two days over a threshold that most companies don't even know exists."

### HK / market fit (20s)
> "Hong Kong is the ideal beachhead. Every HKEX-listed group, every regional HQ, every fintech with cross-border payment flows has this problem — especially as OECD Pillar Two changes the game for HK's territorial tax advantage. The Fintech Association's own members are exactly our market."

### Business model (10s)
> "SaaS, per entity per jurisdiction. The advisor still reviews and signs off — we just make sure they arrive with the right information already on the table."

### Close (5s)
> "Every company in this room with cross-border operations is either exposed or doesn't know it yet. Tributary closes that gap."

## Delivery notes

- **Slow down on the hook.** She's been in those calls. Let the recognition land before you move on.
- **During the demo:** don't narrate every click. Say what it means for the user, not what the code is doing.
- **"The advisor still reviews and signs off"** — say this clearly. She will think about liability. Pre-empt it.
- **Don't over-explain the tech.** If she asks, you answer. Don't front-load it.
